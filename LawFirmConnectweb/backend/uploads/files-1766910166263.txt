Chemotherapy-Related Complications Management:
Neutropenic Fever:
•	Definition: Absolute neutrophil count <500 + fever ≥38.3°C
•	Protocol: Empiric antibiotics within 1 hour
•	Common regimen: Piperacillin-tazobactam or Meropenem
Nausea & Vomiting:
•	Graded 0-4 (CTCAE v5.0)
•	Prevention: 5-HT3 antagonist + dexamethasone + NK1 antagonist
•	Rescue: Additional antiemetics
Mucositis:
•	Grading: CTCAE v5.0
•	Management: Oral rinses, topical agents, pain relief
•	Prevention: Cryotherapy (ice chips) during 5-FU infusion
Tumor-Related Complications:
Superior Vena Cava Syndrome (SVCS):
•	Presentation: Facial edema, venous distension, dyspnea
•	Management: Head elevation, diuretics, emergency chemotherapy/radiotherapy
Spinal Cord Compression:
•	Presentation: Back pain, weakness, sensory loss
•	Management: Emergency MRI, corticosteroids, radiation/surgery
Brain Metastases:
•	Incidence: 20-40% of cancer patients

Part 4: HIPAA Compliance & Data Security
4.1 HIPAA Privacy Rule Implementation
Protected Health Information (PHI) Definition:
PHI includes any information in medical records or health histories that can identify an individual:
Direct Identifiers (Must be removed/restricted):
•	Names
•	Medical record numbers
•	Identification numbers
•	Phone numbers
•	Email addresses
•	Social security numbers
•	Account numbers
•	Certificate numbers
•	Vehicle identification numbers
•	Device identifiers
•	Photograph or face images
•	Any other unique identifier
Quasi-Identifiers (Sensitive context):
•	Age (if >89 years)
•	Dates (admission, discharge, birth)
•	Geographic information (zip codes)
•	Occupation
Permitted Uses & Disclosures:
Without Authorization:
•	Patient care treatment (coordinated care)
•	Healthcare operations (billing, QA, training)
•	Public health activities (disease reporting)
•	Law enforcement (with proper warrant)
•	Judicial/administrative proceedings
With Authorization:
•	Any disclosure not mentioned above
•	Research studies (separate consent)
•	Marketing (explicit opt-out available)
•	Third-party request
Patient Rights:
Right	Implementation
Access	Patients can request medical records (within 30 days)
Amendment	Patients can request correction of errors
Accounting of Disclosure	Patient can request who accessed their records
Restriction	Patient can request to limit uses/disclosures
Confidential Communication	Request for specific communication methods
Breach Notification	Informed within 60 days if breach occurs

4.2 Security Rule Implementation
Administrative Safeguards:
Workforce Security:
•	Workforce authorization: Role-based access levels
o	Level 1: Read-only (Reception staff)
o	Level 2: Read-write clinical data (Nurses, doctors)
o	Level 3: Full system access (IT, administrators)
o	Level 4: Audit/compliance access (Compliance officer)
•	User identification & authentication:
o	Username/password (minimum 12 characters, complexity required)
o	Multi-factor authentication for sensitive roles
o	Periodic password changes (90 days)
o	Account lockout after 3 failed attempts
•	Termination procedures:
o	Immediate system access removal
o	Equipment return verification
o	Data deletion confirmation
o	Exit interview documentation
Information Access Management:
•	Access based on minimum necessary principle
•	Role-based access control (RBAC)
•	Separation of duties (no single person can process from start to finish)
•	Regular audit of access logs (monthly)
Physical Safeguards:
Facility Access Controls:
•	Main server room: Badge access + biometric authentication
•	Restricted areas: Locked doors, visitor logs
•	Pharmacy: Narcotics in double-locked cabinet
•	Laboratory: Area restriction for specimen storage
•	Monitoring: CCTV in all patient care areas (24/7), recorded 30 days
Workstation Security:
•	Screensaver: 15-minute auto-lock
•	Monitor: Privacy screen for sensitive areas
•	Data deletion: Secure wipe using DOD 5220.22-M standard
•	Disconnection: Auto-timeout after 30 minutes inactivity
Technical Safeguards:
Encryption:
•	Data at rest: AES-256 for database encryption
•	Data in transit: TLS 1.2+ for all communications
•	USB drives: Mandatory encryption for any portable media
•	Backup: Encrypted copies stored off-site
Access Controls:
•	Log-in procedures: User ID + password + MFA
•	Automatic logoff: 30 minutes of inactivity
•	Encryption/decryption: Automatically applied
•	Audit controls: All access logged with timestamp
Audit Trail:
•	Every PHI access recorded: User ID, timestamp, action, record accessed
•	Monthly audit reports: Unusual access patterns flagged
•	Retention: 12 months of audit logs maintained
•	Investigation: Any suspicious activity triggers alert
Data Integrity:
•	Checksums: Verify data hasn't been altered
•	Non-repudiation: Digital signatures for critical documents
•	Regular backups: Daily incremental, weekly full
•	Disaster recovery: RTO 4 hours, RPO 1 hour
4.3 Breach Notification Protocol
Breach Definition:
Unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises security/privacy.
Assessment for Breach Risk (HIPAA breach band matrix):
Factor	Low Risk	High Risk
Encrypted?	Yes	No
Likely accessed?	No	Yes
Mitigation?	Implemented	Not available

Notification Timeline:
•	Discovery of breach: Document immediately
•	Individual notification: Within 60 days of discovery
•	Media notification (if >500 residents): Without unreasonable delay
•	HHS notification: Within 60 days to Secretary
Notification Content (Must Include):
•	Description of breach
•	Date range of exposure
•	Types of PHI involved
•	Steps individuals should take
•	Hospital's investigative steps
•	Prevention measures implemented
•	Contact information for questions
Recent Breach Examples (Hypothetical):
Breach 1: Unencrypted USB drive with 500 patient records lost during transport
•	Risk assessment: High (unencrypted, probably accessed)
•	Action: Notify all 500 patients, provide credit monitoring
•	Cost: ~500,000 INR (notification + monitoring) + regulatory fines
Breach 2: Unauthorized database query by former employee (accessed, then immediately detected)
•	Risk assessment: Medium (accessed, but immediate detection)
•	Action: Notify 45 affected patients, enhance monitoring
•	Cost: ~180,000 INR + regulatory scrutiny

Part 10: Departmental Protocols & Clinical Pathways (Detailed)
10.1 Sepsis Protocol
Definition: SIRS + Suspected/confirmed infection
SIRS Criteria (≥2):
•	Temperature: >38°C or <36°C
•	HR: >90 bpm
•	RR: >20 breaths/min
•	WBC: >12,000/µL or <4,000/µL
Initial Assessment (First Hour - "Golden Hour"):
1.	Blood cultures: Before antibiotics (2 sets from different sites)
2.	Imaging: CXR, ultrasound, CT as indicated
3.	Lactate level: Baseline measurement
4.	CBC, CMP, coagulation profile: Baseline labs
5.	Urinalysis & culture: If UTI suspected
Management Bundle (0-3 hours):
•	Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloids for hypotension/lactate >4
•	Vasopressors: Target MAP ≥65 mmHg (norepinephrine first-line)
•	Source control: Identify & treat infection source
•	Antibiotics: Within 1 hour of recognition
o	Empiric regimen based on likely source:
	Community-acquired pneumonia: Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin
	UTI/intra-abdominal: Cephalosporin + Metronidazole
	Meningitis: Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Ampicillin
	Unknown: Carbapenems (Meropenem)
•	Glucose control: Target 150-180 mg/dL
•	Corticosteroids: Only if refractory hypotension despite fluids/vasopressors
Monitoring:
•	Hourly vitals × 3 hours
•	Serial lactate (repeat at 3 & 6 hours)
•	Urine output: Target ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr
•	Reassess source of infection regularly
ICU Admission Criteria:
•	Persistent hypotension despite fluids
•	Lactate >4 mmol/L
•	Need for vasopressors
•	Altered mental status
•	Organ dysfunction
Mortality Prediction (Sequential Organ Failure Assessment - SOFA score):
SOFA Score	Predicted Mortality
<7	10%
7-9	25-50%
10-12	50-75%
>12	>75%

10.2 Acute Stroke Protocol
Definition: Sudden neurological deficit suspicious for stroke
Presentation:
•	Facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
•	Onset documented to last known normal time
Pre-hospital (EMS):
•	Notify ED of incoming stroke alert (15 min ETA)
•	Obtain precise time of onset
•	Establish IV access en route
•	Blood glucose point-of-care test
ED Arrival - Stroke Alert Activation:
Door-to-CT: <25 minutes
1.	NIHSS score assessment
2.	Fingerstick glucose check
3.	Non-contrast CT head (or MRI)
4.	Basic metabolic panel, CBC, coagulation
CT Result Interpretation (15 minutes from arrival):
Ischemic stroke (No hemorrhage on CT):
•	Onset <4.5 hours: tPA eligible
•	Onset 4.5-24 hours: Thrombectomy eligible (select criteria)
•	Onset >24 hours: No acute therapy
tPA Protocol (if eligible):
•	Inclusion: NIHSS 4-22, no contraindications
•	Exclusion: Recent surgery, active bleeding, INR >1.7
•	Dose: 0.9 mg/kg IV bolus (10% as 1 min bolus, remainder over 60 min)
•	Monitoring: Neurological checks every 15 minutes × 2 hours, then hourly × 24 hours
Hemorrhagic stroke (Blood on CT):
•	Immediate neurosurgery consult
•	ICU admission
•	Reverse anticoagulation (if on warfarin: FFP or PCC)
•	Blood pressure control: SBP <140 mmHg target
Post-thrombolysis Care:
•	ICU admission
•	Repeat CT at 24 hours (rule out hemorrhagic transformation)
•	DVT prophylaxis (sequential compression devices, not pharmacological in first 24 hours)
•	Aspirin: Start at 24 hours post-thrombolysis
Discharge Planning (After Acute Phase):
•	Aspirin 150 mg OD
•	Statin (if not contraindicated)
•	Hypertension management
•	Diabetes screening/optimization
•	Physical/occupational therapy referral
•	Outpatient neurology follow-up (2 weeks)

10.3 Chest Pain Triage Protocol
Chief Complaint: Chest discomfort/pain
Initial Triage (First 5 minutes):
•	Vital signs (HR, BP, RR, SpO2, Temperature)
•	12-lead ECG (target: within 10 minutes of arrival)
•	IV access
•	Oxygen if SpO2 <94%
ECG Interpretation:
•	STEMI (ST elevation >1 mm in ≥2 contiguous leads): Activate cath lab, prepare for PCI
•	Non-STEMI (T wave inversion, ST depression): Cardiology consult, troponin serial
•	Normal/unchanged: Risk stratification protocol
Risk Stratification (HEART score):
Factor	Points
History: Mild/typical	0
History: Intermediate/atypical	1
History: High-risk	2
ECG: Normal	0
ECG: Nonspecific changes	1
ECG: Ischemic changes	2
Age: <45	0
Age: 45-65	1
Age: >65	2
Risk factors: 0-1	0
Risk factors: 2-3	1
Risk factors: >3	2
Troponin: Normal	0
Troponin: 0-3× upper limit normal	1
Troponin: >3× upper limit normal	2

Score Interpretation:
•	Low risk (0-3): Discharge with outpatient follow-up
•	Intermediate risk (4-6): Observation, serial troponin, stress test
•	High risk (7+): Admission, cardiology consult, angiography
Troponin Measurement:
•	High-sensitivity troponin: At 0 & 3 hours
•	If both normal: ACS ruled out
•	If elevated at either time: Admit for further management
Medical Management (Initial):
•	Aspirin: 325 mg chewed
•	P2Y12 inhibitor: Clopidogrel 600 mg or Prasugrel 60 mg
•	Beta-blocker: Metoprolol 50 mg (if HR >60, SBP >90)
•	Nitroglycerin: SL 0.6 mg, repeat every 5 min (if SBP >90)
•	Statin: Atorvastatin 80 mg
•	ACE inhibitor: Lisinopril 5 mg (if LVEF <40%)
